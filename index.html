Property and Casualty Insurance

What is Property and Casualty Insurance

Property and casualty insurance is a vital component in the financial well-being of individuals, businesses, and communities. It provides protection against a wide range of risks, ensuring financial stability and peace of mind.

  • This type of insurance is designed to safeguard policyholders against unexpected events and ensure that they can recover from financial setbacks.
  • Property and casualty insurance is essential because it allows individuals and businesses to protect their assets, minimize financial losses, and maintain their standard of living or operations following an unexpected event.
  • It also helps to create a more stable and resilient society by spreading the financial risk associated with accidents, natural disasters, and other unforeseen events.

What is PPM(Project Portfolio Management)

PPM is the tool where we are creating defects. For e.g. JIRA, ServiceNow, HP ALM, Azure DevOps. PPM is a system or methodology used by organizations to manage and oversee projects and their portfoloios. In this context, it specifically refers to a tool where "defects" are created.

What is CSR(Client Service Request)

A Client Service Request (CSR) is a formal request made by a customer to a service provider for assistance, support, or a specific action related to a product or service. It is commonly used in industries such as IT, telecom, banking, and customer support to track and manage customer inquiries efficiently. Key Aspects of a CSR - Request Submission – Customers submit a CSR through various channels like email, phone, or online portals. - Tracking & Resolution – The request is logged, assigned to a relevant team, and tracked until resolution. - Service-Level Agreements (SLAs) – Many CSRs operate under SLAs, ensuring timely responses and resolutions.

CSR is a formal request from a client or for a service, change or resolution of an issue. In this context, it seems to be a broader term that encompasses various types of requests that might lead to development work or support activities.

Policy Type

  • BOP: A Business Owner’s Policy combines several essential coverages into one package, typically for small to medium-sized businesses.
  • Automobile: - Covers vehicles used for business purposes. This can include cars, trucks, or vans that are owned, leased, or rented by the business. It typically protects against liability, collision, and comprehensive damages.
  • Workers Compensation: - Mandatory in many regions, this policy provides medical benefits and wage replacement to employees who are injured on the job. In return, employees generally waive the right to sue their employer for negligence.
  • General Liability: - Covers a wide range of common business risks—like bodily injury, property damage, and even advertising injury (such as copyright infringement or slander). This is the cornerstone of most business insurance plans.
  • Inland Marine: - Despite the name, this policy has nothing to do with ships. It covers goods, tools, and equipment in transit or stored offsite. It’s especially useful for businesses that frequently transport valuable items.
  • Property: Protects the physical assets of your business, like buildings, inventory, equipment, and sometimes even outdoor signage. It typically covers losses from fire, theft, and some natural disasters.

DAR

DAR(Defect Assessment Request)

A Defect Assessment Request typically refers to a formal process in software development or quality assurance where a team identifies and evaluates a defect (or bug) found in a system, application, or product.
Purpose: To formally document and initiate the evaluation of a defect, including its impact, severity, and required actions.
Common Use Cases:

  • During software testing, when a tester finds a bug and wants it reviewed by developers or QA leads.
  • In production environments, when users or support teams report issues that need technical assessment.
Workflow:
  • Submission
  • Assessment
  • Classification
  • Resolution Plan
